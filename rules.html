Mine Trap

Section 01

Overview

The promise of the game in one pass before players touch the cards.

The Story

You are trapped in a collapsed coal mine. Somewhere in the center of the mine is a steam-powered elevator, but the furnace is fading and the passages are unstable. Your team must explore the mine, find enough coal, uncover the elevator, and gather every pawn there before the air runs out.

Objective

Get all pawns to the Elevator and gather enough Coal to power the furnace.

Standard Target

Use 12 Coal for standard playtesting. Use 10 for an easier game or 14 for a harder game.

Loss

If the air marker reaches zero before the team escapes, all players lose together.

Escape Check If all pawns are on the Elevator, players may immediately deposit any Coal from their inventories into the furnace pile. If the furnace contains 12 or more Coal, all players win.

Key Card Families

Pickaxe

Used to gain buried items and Dig Deep into the current space or an adjacent two-card stack.

Coal

Coal powers the Elevator furnace. The current folder includes three Coal art variants for texture and discovery interest; under the current playtest rules, they all play the same.

Coal Pile

A table location for deposited Coal. It functions like a discard pile for Coal that players have stoked into the Elevator furnace during the game.

Air Counter

Choose the Air Counter card that matches player count, then place a marker pawn on the starting air value.

Removed from Game

An off-grid pile locator for cards permanently removed from the current play session.

Rope

Placed as rotated board infrastructure. It can help with pits and rivers, and is not washed away by water.

Tool Chest

When gained, remove it from play and draw from the Tool Chest pile.

Air Masks

Optional Tool Chest item. It may be held and discarded at any time to move the air counter back 1 space.

Spider Repellent

Repellent variants add visual interest but play the same. Repellent can be washed away by River flooding.

Cavern

A pit-like setback with a new route out of the mine; when escaped, it can reposition a pawn across the board.

Elevator

The goal location. Gather all pawns here and stoke the furnace with enough Coal to escape.

Giant Spider

A moving setback unless it is trapping a pawn on its space. Multiple spider versions are available so the prototype can support an easier spider mix.

Section 03

Setup

A teachable setup flow for building the hidden mine.

Build The Mine

Prepare the base grid

Shuffle the base cards and place them in a 5x5 grid with the River side down. Leave space between cards for rotated cards such as Rope, Repellent, and moving Spiders.

Set aside the Elevator

Remove the Elevator from the mine deck. It will be placed as the bottom mine card in one randomly chosen space of the center 3x3.

Randomize Coal and High Rubble

Pull 6 Coal cards and 6 High Rubble cards. Shuffle them together, place 4 facedown in the Removed from Game pile, then shuffle the remaining 8 back into the mine deck.

Build the center

Randomly choose one space in the center 3x3 and place the Elevator there facedown as the bottom mine card. Draw 8 random mine cards and place one facedown as the bottom mine card in each of the other center spaces.

When completing the mine, make sure the Elevator space receives at least one facedown mine card on top of the Elevator. The Elevator is always buried in the center 3x3 and is always on the bottom layer of mine cards.

Complete the mine

Layer the remaining mine cards over the 5x5 grid, slightly offsetting stacked cards so players can see where spaces contain more than one card.

Build the Tool Chest pile

From the Tool Chest cards, place Pickaxe, Spider Repellent, and Rope cards into a facedown Tool Chest pile according to player count. Place unused Tool Chest cards in the Removed from Game pile.

Prepare inventories

Give each player 2 Pickaxe cards and 1 Coal card from the starting item cards. Place any leftover starting item cards in the Removed from Game pile.

Each player draws 1 card from the Tool Chest pile. Then each player places all of their cards side by side and face up in front of them. This row of cards is that player's inventory.

Choose starting spaces

Players may start on any edge space. Multiple players may start on the same edge space if they choose to do so strategically.

Opening Scene Read aloud: "You are trapped in a collapsed coal mine and the elevator out is buried underneath another card in one of the center 9 spaces. The goal is to gather 12 coal and get everyone to that elevator so it can be powered and you can escape. You must all cooperate to win and you will have a limited number of rounds to breathe! Fortunately, you have some items in your inventory to help."

Section 04

Core Rules

The shared procedure that drives turns, air, discovery, and hazards.

Turn Structure

  • Movement is optional. A player does not have to move.
  • Moving nowhere is a legal move.
  • Each player must resolve two actions each turn.
  • For each unused action, the player must Stow Item by flipping one face-up inventory card facedown.
  • Rescuing another pawn from an adjacent space can be done as the player's move.
Locked

Inventory Cooldown

  • All player inventory cards are visible to the table.
  • When all cards in a player's inventory are face down, refresh immediately by flipping all inventory cards face up.
  • All gained item cards enter inventory face up.
Locked

Air

At the end of each round, reduce air by 1. If the air marker reaches zero, all players lose.

Burning Coal always depletes air by 1 round.

Progress Circles

Some setbacks place a pawn on progress circles to represent engagement in a continued activity.

While your pawn is on an arrowless progress circle, your move must only be used to advance your pawn to the next sequential circle, and your actions are restricted to actions that do not involve adjacent spaces.

Progress circles with one or more arrows do not restrict moves or actions in the direction that the arrows point. If there is a legal move from the final progress circle, the player may choose to spend an action before moving away.

End Of Round

1. Deplete Air

Move the air marker down by 1. If it reaches zero, the game ends in a loss.

2. Flood

Flooding happens as one wave per round. Advanced rules may allow chained flooding.

3. Spiders

In alphabetical order by name, move each revealed Spider that contains no pawns.

Section 05

Card Rules

Locked card behaviors translated from designer answers. Some card text still needs final layout wording.

Coal And Furnace

Action: Stoke the Furnace. While your pawn is on the Elevator, you may use 1 action to deposit 1 Coal from your inventory into the furnace pile. Deposited Coal counts toward the final total.

Coal in inventory also counts during the Escape Check, including face-down Coal cards.

The Coal cards currently include three visual variants. For the current rules draft, all Coal cards play the same and count as one Coal unless a later component-count pass explicitly changes this.

Coal Pile

The Coal Pile is not a Coal variant. It is the table location where players place Coal cards they deposit into the Elevator furnace throughout the game.

Coal in the Coal Pile counts toward the final Escape Check.

Removed from Game Pile

The Removed from Game card is an off-board pile locator. Place any card that is permanently removed from the current play session here instead of returning it to a deck, inventory, discard, or board space.

Pickaxe: Dig Deep

2 Actions: Dig Deep. Choose your current space or an adjacent space with two unrotated mine cards. Flip two Pickaxes in your inventory facedown to swap the two cards in that space. Reveal the new top card.

If the revealed card has a Forced rule, resolve it immediately if applicable.

Dig Deep may target a space containing a Spider, Rope, Repellent, standing pawn, or trapped pawn. Dig Deep swaps only the mine cards in that space; pawns remain on top of the space, and overlay cards such as Rope, Repellent, or a free rotated Spider remain in place unless another rule moves them.

You may not Dig Deep your current space while your pawn is on a progress circle.

Rivers And Flooding

Flooding happens as one wave per round. Base cards with rotated cards on them are considered empty for flooding. When a River floods that space, remove any rotated card there except Rope.

River spaces remain legal Dig Deep targets.

Rope And Repellent

Rope and Repellent stay on the board as rotated cards after being placed, unless Repellent is washed away by a River. Taking one back with "You may take it with you" is a free action.

Rope is not washed away by newly revealed water. Repellent can be washed away. Rope or Repellent may be placed even if the card underneath is unrevealed.

When Rope is placed on a River, it creates a crossing that allows pawns to move across that River using the Rope. Rope does not stop forced River movement.

Spiders

Spiders do not move when revealed. When a Spider is revealed, it stays on that space. If one or more pawns are on the same space as a Spider, the Spider traps those pawns immediately and does not move.

During the Spiders step at the end of each round, move revealed Spiders in alphabetical order by name. Only move a Spider if it contains no pawns.

A Spider may move to one of the 8 surrounding spaces. It cannot move to a space topped by another Spider, a River, a Repellent, or the Elevator. From the remaining legal spaces, move the Spider to the space with the highest number shown on the edge of that space's base card. Players may slightly move the cards covering a base card to see its edge numbers. If there are no legal spaces, the Spider does not move.

A free moved Spider is placed rotated across the card it is over so players know they may lift it to see any revealed card beneath it. If a Spider moves onto a space with one or more pawns, place those pawns on the first progress circle; the Spider is now trapping them and should be aligned normally.

If a pawn moves onto a space topped by a rotated Spider, that Spider traps the pawn immediately. Place the pawn on the first progress circle and align the Spider normally.

Spider Nest

Spiders use base-card edge numbers to choose movement. When a Spider is forced back to its nest, it must return there. If the nest is blocked for any reason, remove that Spider from the game.

Current Coal Variants

The Coal cards use three artwork variants to make coal discoveries feel less repetitive. Under the current playtest rules, all Coal variants play the same.

Current Spider Variants

These cards share the Giant Spider system, but the prototype now includes multiple named versions so the final game can tune difficulty and variety.

Spider Repellent Variants

The Spider Repellent cards use different artwork for interest, but they play the same unless a later rules pass says otherwise.

Section 06

Example Scenarios

Rules examples to make edge cases teachable. These can become diagrams later.

Example: Finding The Elevator

A player is adjacent to a two-card stack in the center 3x3. The team suspects the Elevator may be buried there.

  1. The player flips two Pickaxes facedown to Dig Deep.
  2. The two cards in that adjacent stack are swapped.
  3. The new top card is revealed.
  4. If the revealed card has a Forced rule, resolve it immediately if applicable.

Example: Flooding A Rotated Card

During the Flood step, a River floods a base card that has a rotated Repellent card on it.

  1. The space is considered empty for flooding.
  2. The base card is flipped to its River side.
  3. The Repellent is washed away and removed.
  4. If the rotated card had been Rope, the Rope would remain.

Example: Escaping Together

All pawns are on the Elevator. The furnace pile contains 9 Coal. Players have 3 more Coal in inventories.

  1. The team performs the Escape Check.
  2. Players immediately deposit inventory Coal into the furnace pile.
  3. The furnace contains 12 Coal.
  4. All players win.

Example: Spider At Its Nest

A Spider is forced back to its nest, but the nest is blocked by a card or condition the Spider cannot enter.

  1. The Spider must attempt to return to its nest.
  2. If it cannot return home for any reason, remove it from the game.
  3. If the nest is open, place the Spider there.
Section 07

Alternate And Advanced Rules

Optional levers for difficulty once the standard game is stable.

Coal Difficulty

Easier game: require 10 Coal. Standard game: require 12 Coal. Harder game: require 14 Coal.

Chained Flooding

Advanced River rule: instead of one flood wave per round, continue flooding until no more spaces can flood.

Air Restoration

By default, air does not recover during the main game. Air-restoring cards should be added only as optional variants.

Optional Air Pocket

Add 1 Air Pocket card to the center group with the Elevator if you want one extra surprise benefit in the mine.

When Air Pocket is first revealed, restore one air on the counter, up to the starting air value. Then leave the card revealed in its space.

This can make a Dig Deep reveal feel exciting even when the team was hoping to find the Elevator.

Optional Air Masks

Shuffle 1 Air Masks card into the Tool Chest pile if you want the team to have one holdable emergency air option.

As an item, Air Masks may be kept in a player's inventory and used at any time. When used, discard it and move the air counter back 1 space, up to the starting air value.

This variant gives players a small timing decision: use the air immediately, or save it for the final escape push.

Tool Chest Tuning

Easier game: add 1 Pickaxe or 1 Rope to the Tool Chest pile. Harder game: remove 1 Spider Repellent from the Tool Chest pile.

Section 08

Designer Answers

Recent clarifications now folded into the standard rules.

Resolved In This Pass

Elevator Placement

The Elevator is always in the center 3x3, always buried under another mine card, and always on the bottom layer of mine cards in its space.

Spider Reveal And Movement

A revealed Spider traps pawns on its space immediately and does not move. Spider movement happens only during the end-of-round Spiders step.

Dig Deep Edge Cases

Dig Deep may target spaces containing a Spider, Rope, Repellent, standing pawn, or trapped pawn. Dig Deep swaps only mine cards; pawns stay on top of the space.

Rope On River

Rope lets pawns move across a River using the Rope. It does not stop forced River movement.

Progress Circle Restrictions

Use the Progress Circles reference card: arrowless circles restrict movement to advancing and restrict actions to non-adjacent actions; arrows remove restrictions in the directions they point.
